How To Open a Dispensary In Alabama

Overview

Interested in opening a dispensary in Alabama? This guide covers the knowledge, cost, and process required to start a cannabis business in the state.

Alabama's Medical Marijuana Legislation

In March 2021, Alabama proposed SB46, also known as the Darren Wesley 'Ato' Hall Compassion Act, to establish medical marijuana regulations. The Alabama Medical Cannabis Commission (AMCC) was created to oversee licensing and patient registration.

Available Medical Marijuana License Types

  • Cultivation License
  • Processing License
  • Transporting License
  • Testing License
  • Dispensing License
  • Integrated Facility License

Licenses require a non-refundable application fee of $2,500, and fees range between $10,000 and $50,000 depending on the license type.

How Much Does It Cost to Open a Dispensary in Alabama?

Opening a medical marijuana dispensary in Alabama involves an initial application fee of $2,500. Additional licensing and development costs range from $35,000 to $75,000, while capital investments for business operations can reach $400,000 to over $1,000,000.

Qualifying Medical Conditions for Patients

  • Anxiety or panic disorder
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Cancer-related nausea, weight loss, or chronic pain
  • Epilepsy and seizure disorders
  • Fibromyalgia
  • HIV/AIDS-related nausea or weight loss
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
  • Multiple sclerosis-related spasticity
  • Terminal illnesses

History of Medical Marijuana in Alabama

The Alabama Medical Cannabis Commission was formed in 2019 to study marijuana legalization. In 2020, SB165 passed through the state senate, allowing medical cannabis consumption via oral pills, transdermal patches, oils, and gummies while prohibiting smoking or vaping raw plant material.
