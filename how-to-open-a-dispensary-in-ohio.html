How to open a Recreational Dispensary in Ohio

Ohio Marijuanas Law

Want to learn how to open a Recreational Dispensary in Ohio? Issue 2, introduced into Ohio Marijuanas Law, has a detailed framework for adult-use cannabis licenses, emphasizing a diverse range of opportunities within the cannabis industry. The legislation specifically mentions licenses like the Level III Adult-Use Cultivator and Adult-Use Dispensary, with a notable focus on supporting social equity applicants. This initiative is designed to ensure fair representation and opportunities for historically marginalized communities and include a social equity program in the burgeoning cannabis market. Read the Initiative Petition, An Act to Control and Regulate Adult Use Cannabis.

In addition to these, the state anticipates the introduction of more varied licenses to encompass a broader spectrum of the cannabis industry. These anticipated licenses include Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 Cultivator licenses, which will cater to different scales of cannabis cultivation operations. Laboratory Testing licenses are also expected, which will play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and quality of cannabis products through rigorous testing protocols. Furthermore, Processing licenses will allow entities to handle the transformation of raw cannabis into various consumable forms, expanding the market's product range.

A notable aspect of the Ohio cannabis legislation is its allowance for organizations to hold multiple licenses, providing a significant opportunity for business expansion within the industry. Additionally, they can possess both a cultivator and a processing license. This provision facilitates vertical integration, allowing businesses to control multiple stages of the supply chain, from cultivation and processing to retail. This approach could lead to greater efficiencies, better quality control, and potentially lower costs for consumers. According to the Act, no person shall possess more than eight Ohio recreational dispensary licenses or one of each license type for a single business entity, except a level III Adult Use Cultivator license. This comprehensive licensing structure in Ohio not only aims to regulate and maintain quality in the adult-use cannabis market but also opens the door for diverse business ventures and equitable participation in this growing industry.

Ohio Division of Cannabis Control

With the passing of Issue 2, the Ohio Department of Commerce and Division of Cannabis Control is set to establish and commence the licensure process within 9 months. This will provide them with the necessary time to determine the rules and regulations associated with opening a cannabis business in Ohio. The ballot included provisions for offering 50 new Ohio Recreational Dispensary Licenses and 40 Level III Adult Use Cultivation Licenses. However, it is anticipated that more licenses will be made available once the division is fully established. The direct responsibilities of the Division include licensing, regulating, investigating, and penalizing adult-use cannabis operators who do not adhere to the rules and regulations put forward by the Division.

Ohio Medical Marijuana Program

On June 9, 2016, Ohio Governor Kasich signed HB 523 into law, authorizing the creation of a new medical marijuana framework in Ohio. The Ohio Board of Commerce and Board of Pharmacy accepted applications in 2017, awarded provisional medical marijuana cultivator licenses in November 2017, provisional medical marijuana dispensary licenses in June 2018, and initial provisional medical marijuana processor licenses in August 2018 with more forthcoming.

The licenses available during the Medical Marijuana licensing round were as follows:

  • Medical marijuana cultivators
  • Medical marijuana processors (extraction and manufacturing)
  • Medical marijuana dispensaries
